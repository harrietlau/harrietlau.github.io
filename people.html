Manar Al Asad, 1st year PhD candidate

I am interested in most problem concerning the evolution of planets over geologic time scales. My current interest, formulated with the guidance of Dr. Lau, is to understand the interactions between the motions of solid rocks in Earth's mantle with the fluid outer core (which is responsible for generating the geomagnetic field), over the majority of Earth's history: how one shaped the other, or both shaped each other, and how the changing properties of the Earth (e.g. length of day, heat distribution, supercontinents) change the structure of the magnetic fieldâ€¦ you know, easy stuff. The details of this relationship, or the right questions to ask in attempt to understand it, is what I plan to dedicate the next chapter of my scientific journey (my PhD) towards. Previously, I dabbled in exploration geophysics, solar wind interactions with Mercury's magnetosphere, and small body exploration through NASA's OSIRIS-REx mission.
